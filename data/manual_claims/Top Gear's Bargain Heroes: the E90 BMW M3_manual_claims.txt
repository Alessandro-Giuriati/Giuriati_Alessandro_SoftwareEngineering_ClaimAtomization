The BMW M3 is regarded as both a “hero” and an “icon” among performance cars.
The BMW M3 brought high-performance rear-wheel-drive cars to the mainstream.
The BMW M3 combines characteristics of a hot hatch and a sports car in a practical body.
The BMW M3 evolved over 31 years from a four-cylinder coupe into a turbocharged saloon.
The BMW M3 has had multiple variants including convertibles and track-focused models.
The E90-generation BMW M3 is the only M3 to use a V8 engine.
The E90-generation BMW M3 was sold between 2007 and 2013.
The E90-generation BMW M3 was available in three body styles.
The E90-generation BMW M3 offered a choice of two gearboxes.
Used E90-generation BMW M3 models can be purchased for under £20,000.
The fourth-generation BMW M3 marked a transition to a more mature design.
The shift from six to eight cylinders increased the M3’s price above £50,000.
The E90-generation M3 uses a 414bhp 4.0-litre V8 engine.
The E90-generation M3 was available with a six-speed manual or a seven-speed DCT automatic gearbox.
The E90-generation M3 accelerates from 0–60 mph in under five seconds.
The E90-generation M3 accelerates from 0–100 mph in about ten seconds.
The E90-generation M3 has a limited top speed of 155 mph.
The E92 coupe was the initial launch model of this generation.
The E93 convertible and E90 saloon followed shortly after the E92 coupe.
Approximately 8,262 E90-generation M3s were sold in the UK.
The coupe accounted for about two-thirds of UK sales.
The coupe version weighs approximately 1,655 kg.
The convertible weighs about 150 kg more than the coupe.
The saloon version is considered the rarest mainstream body style in the UK.
Only 625 E90 M3 saloons were sold in the UK.
The M3 GTS produced 444bhp and only seven units came to the UK.
The saloon version lacks the carbon roof featured on the coupe.
All E90-generation M3 models feature design elements such as a power dome bonnet, side vents, flared arches, and quad exhausts.
The interior includes M-specific controls such as a programmable “M” button.
The “M” button allows drivers to customize settings including stability control, suspension, and gearshift response.
In normal mode, the E90 M3 behaves similarly to a standard BMW 3 Series.
The E90 M3 features hydraulic steering.
The E90 M3 uses a naturally aspirated V8 engine.
The engine can rev beyond 8,000 rpm.
The DCT gearbox was more popular than the manual over the model’s lifecycle.
The E90 M3 delivers 414bhp and 295 lb-ft of torque to the rear wheels.
The car’s power delivery builds progressively rather than delivering instant turbocharged torque.
The engine’s redline reaches approximately 8,300 rpm.
The E90 M3 can return to a refined, comfortable driving mode for everyday use.
The E90 M3’s throttle bodies are a known common failure point.
Faulty throttle bodies should be replaced in pairs.
Replacement throttle bodies cost approximately £650 each.
Replacing both throttle bodies typically costs around £1,800 including VAT.
The engine is prone to bottom-end failure if poorly maintained.
Improper maintenance or aggressive driving when cold can shorten engine lifespan.
Well-maintained engines can exceed 100,000 miles without major issues.
Poorly maintained engines may require major work at around 50,000 miles.
An engine rebuild can cost approximately £8,000.
The car uses a condition-based servicing system.
Annual oil changes cost around £250.
Major services every 40,000–50,000 miles cost about £700.
Running-in service fluid changes are recommended at 1,000 miles.
The DCT gearbox may develop oil leaks costing at least £500 to repair.
Exhaust downpipes may leak and manifolds may crack, particularly on modified cars.
Fuel economy ranges from about 18 mpg when driven hard to around 30 mpg when driven gently.
Typical combined fuel economy is around 22–24 mpg.
Oil consumption is approximately one litre every 4,000–5,000 miles.
Recommended oil is 10W-60 fully synthetic approved for BMW M engines.
Premium tyres cost around £250 each.
Tyres can last 15,000–20,000 miles with careful driving.
Brake performance is considered strong but may require upgrades for track use.
Early 2007 E92 M3 coupes start at just under £17,000.
At £20,000, buyers can access all body styles and gearbox options.
At £25,000, many cars have under 50,000 miles and may include Competition Pack upgrades.
The Competition Pack includes lower suspension, 19-inch wheels, and revised ESP tuning.
High-spec or low-mileage examples can approach £30,000.
Limited models such as the M3 GT3 and M3 CRT can exceed £120,000.
