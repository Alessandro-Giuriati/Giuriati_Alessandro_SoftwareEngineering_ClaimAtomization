Microsoft and Meta are planning significant workforce cuts as spending on artificial intelligence increases.
Meta announced it would lay off about 8,000 workers, representing about 10% of its workforce.
Meta confirmed that around 6,000 job vacancies would be left unfilled.
Meta previously told investors that its spending would exceed $160bn in 2026.
Meta’s spending in the prior year was just under $120bn.
Meta is competing with rivals to hire AI experts.
Meta faces large costs for data centres to support technological development.
The announcement continues a trend of job cuts across major US tech firms in recent years.
Microsoft is offering a voluntary redundancy programme.
The Associated Press reported that the programme would affect about 8,750 people in Microsoft’s core US workforce.
The number represents 7% of Microsoft’s total staff.
CNBC reported earlier that the voluntary redundancy scheme exists.
The scheme is scheduled to be rolled out early next month.
CNBC reported that staff were informed of the scheme through a memo from Microsoft’s chief people officer, Amy Coleman.
Amy Coleman stated in the memo that the programme aims to give eligible employees the choice to leave with company support.
