Some RAM variants of the Mac Studio and Mac mini M4 are listed as “currently unavailable” in Apple’s online store when selected.

Apple has not provided a reason for the unavailability of these RAM variants.

Observers believe the unavailability may be due to a current memory crisis.

Some device variants had previously been available only with delays.

Apple removed the 512 GB RAM variant of the Mac Studio in March without replacement.

Configurations that were previously considered standard are now also affected by unavailability.

The 32 GB model of the Mac mini M4 is listed as temporarily unavailable.

The 32 GB Mac mini M4 is also unavailable in most retail stores.

The 64 GB version of the Mac mini M4 Pro is listed as unavailable.

The 128 GB variant of the Mac Studio with M4 Max is listed as unavailable.

Apple has not provided information on why these availability issues exist.

Apple has not indicated when the availability situation will improve.

Availability for these configurations is often better at alternative retailers than at Apple.

Prices for these configurations are usually lower at alternative retailers than at Apple.

One possible explanation for the unavailability is that Apple is preparing new Mac mini and Mac Studio models.

Typically, when new models are being prepared, existing configurations are listed as delayed rather than completely unavailable.

The Mac mini M4 was released in November 2024.

The Mac Studio M4 Max and the M3 Ultra were released in March 2025.
