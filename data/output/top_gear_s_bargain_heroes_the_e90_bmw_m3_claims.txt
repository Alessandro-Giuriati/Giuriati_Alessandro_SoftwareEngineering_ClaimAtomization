Extracted claims:

1. The BMW M3 is presented as a high-performance rear-wheel-drive car that helped bring that type of car into the mainstream.
2. The article says the M3 evolved over 31 years from a four-cylinder coupe into a turbocharged saloon and spawned multiple variants, including convertible and track-focused versions.
3. The E90-generation M3 was sold from 2007 to 2013.
4. The E90-generation M3 was the only M3 generation to use an eight-cylinder engine.
5. The E90 M3 used a highly strung 4.0-litre V8 with 414bhp.
6. The E90 M3 was offered with a six-speed manual gearbox or an optional seven-speed DCT twin-clutch transmission.
7. The E90 M3 could do 0-60mph in under five seconds, 0-100mph in 10 seconds, and had a 155mph limited top speed.
8. The E90-generation M3 launched as the E92 coupe, with the E93 convertible and E90 saloon arriving later.
9. In the UK, coupes accounted for two-thirds of the 8,262 E90-generation M3s sold.
10. The coupe was the lightest body style in this generation at 1,655kg.
11. The saloon was sold in the UK in limited numbers, with 625 examples.
12. The E90 M3 shared visual cues across the range including a bonnet power dome, M-badged side vents, bulging wheel arches, quad exhausts, and a more intricate front bumper than a standard 3 Series.
13. The cabin featured an M-branded gear knob or DCT selector, bespoke dials with a light-up red line, and an M button on the steering wheel.
14. The M button was used to store preferred driving settings such as stability control, suspension firmness, and gearshift response.
15. Without its sport settings engaged, the M3 behaves like a normal BMW 3 Series and is refined and easy to drive.
16. On interesting roads, the E90 M3 is sharp and precise, with hydraulic steering and strong front-end grip.
17. The V8 engine is the standout feature of the car, and its power delivery is naturally aspirated rather than turbocharged.
18. The engine’s sound builds from a low burble to a screaming note beyond 8,000rpm.
19. The DCT gearbox was available on the E90 M3 and was outsold by the manual over the car’s life.
20. The E90 M3 is less intimidating than the current twin-turbo M3, especially in poor road conditions.
21. The car can send 414bhp and 295lb ft to the rear axle.
22. BMW specialist Dan Norris of Munich Legends says the E90 M3 is the best all-round M car BMW has made and that his own car has covered 50,000 hard miles without going wrong.
23. The E90 M3’s two throttle bodies are a known failure point and should be replaced if they have not already been done.
24. If one throttle body fails, both should be replaced together, and the article says the job costs around £1,800 including VAT.
25. The M3’s V8 can suffer bottom-end failure, especially if it is revved hard from cold or poorly maintained.
26. Well-maintained engines can last beyond 100,000 miles, while badly treated ones may need major work at 50,000 miles.
27. The article estimates an engine rebuild could cost about £8,000.
28. The M3 uses a condition-based servicing system.
29. As a guide, the V8 needs annual oil changes at about £250 and a more detailed service every 40,000 to 50,000 miles at about £700.
30. The running-in fluids should be changed at 1,000 miles.
31. The DCT gearbox can leak oil and the repair is said to cost at least £500.
32. The exhaust downpipes can leak and the manifolds can crack, particularly on modified cars or those with aftermarket exhaust work.
33. The E90 M3 typically returns about 18mpg when driven hard, around 22 to 24mpg in mixed use, and up to about 30mpg when driven gently.
34. Premium tyres cost about £250 each and may last 15,000 to 20,000 miles if treated carefully.
35. The article says the E90 M3 is a relatively cost-effective M car to run.
36. Early 2007 E92 M3 coupes start at just under £17,000.
37. At around £20,000, buyers can choose from all body styles and gearboxes.
38. Around £25,000 buys a wider selection including Competition Pack cars and post-facelift examples.
39. Cars near £30,000 tend to have very low mileage or high-spec individual options.
40. The M3 GT3 and M3 CRT cost £120,000 or more.
